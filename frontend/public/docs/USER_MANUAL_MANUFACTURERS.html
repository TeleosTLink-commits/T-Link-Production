5. CoA Lookup

5.1 What is a CoA?

A Certificate of Analysis (CoA) is an official document that certifies the quality, purity, and composition of a chemical sample. It includes:

  • Lot/batch number
  • Test results
  • Certification date
  • Expiration date (if applicable)

5.2 Finding a CoA

  1. From the Dashboard, click CoA Lookup
  2. You'll see a dropdown list of all samples
  3. Select the sample you need
  4. Click Get Certificate

5.3 Viewing Results

If a CoA is available, you'll see:

  • Sample name
  • Lot number
  • Certification date
  • Expiration date (if applicable)
  • Download button

5.4 Downloading the CoA

  1. Click Download
  2. The CoA PDF will either open in a new tab OR download directly to your computer
  3. You can print or save the PDF for your records
üí°
No CoA Found? If no CoA is available for your selected sample, contact support to request one. The CoA may be pending upload, or verify you selected the correct lot number.

7. Requesting a Shipment

7.1 Starting a Request

  1. From the Dashboard, click Request Shipment
  2. The shipment request form will open

7.2 Contact Information

Fill in your contact details:

Field Description Required
First Name Your first name Yes

Company Your company name Optional

7.3 Delivery Address

Enter the destination address for the shipment:

Field Description Required
Street Address Street address, suite/unit number Yes
City Destination city Yes
State/Province State or province code Yes
ZIP/Postal Code Postal code Yes
Country Default: US Yes

Important Ensure the address is accurate. FedEx will validate the address during processing, and incorrect addresses may cause delays.

7.4 Emergency Contact

For safety compliance, an Emergency Contact Phone is required for all shipments. This is the number that can be reached 24/7 in case of a shipping emergency (especially for hazardous materials).

7.5 Selecting Samples

Adding Your First Sample:

  1. Use the dropdown to select a sample (shows chemical name and lot number)
  2. Enter the Quantity Requested (in the appropriate units)
  3. Select the Unit (ml, g, kg, etc.)

Adding Multiple Samples:

  • Click + Add Another Sample to add more samples to the same shipment
  • You can add up to 10 samples per shipment request
  • Click the Remove button next to any sample to delete it

7.6 HazMat Information

When you select a sample, the system automatically displays any hazardous materials information:

  • UN Number - United Nations hazard ID
  • Hazard Class - DOT classification (1-9)
  • Packing Group - Severity level (I, II, or III)
  • Proper Shipping Name - Official DOT name

10. FAQ & Troubleshooting

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I register for an account?

Your email must first be authorized by Ajwa Labs. Contact your representative, then click "Create Account" on the login page.

Q: I forgot my password. What do I do?

Contact support at jhunzie@ajwalabs.com to request a password reset.

Q: Why can't I see my past orders?

The portal shows orders linked to your account. If you recently registered, previous orders may be under a different account. Contact support.

Q: How long does shipping take?

Shipping times depend on the service level:

  • Ground: 3-7 business days
  • Express Saver: 2-3 business days
  • Priority Overnight: Next business day

Q: Can I cancel or modify an order?

Contact lab support immediately. Orders may be modified if they haven't started processing yet.

Q: What if my package arrives damaged?

Contact lab support immediately with photos of the damage. Keep all packaging materials for the carrier's inspection.
